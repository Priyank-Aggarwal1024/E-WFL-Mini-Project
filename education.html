Plastic Bottles

  • Cut plastic bottles into various shapes like flowers, butterflies, or leaves.
  • Paint them using acrylic or spray paint.
  • Attach them to a canvas or a frame to create a colorful wall hanging.

Newspapers or Magazines

  • Roll newspaper or magazine pages into tight tubes to make paper rolls.
  • Arrange and glue the paper rolls into shapes like vases, bowls, or sculptures.
  • Paint or decorate the paper rolls with patterns or colors to enhance their appearance.

Glass Bottles or Jars

  • Clean and remove labels from glass bottles or jars.
  • Paint them with glass paints or coat them with colored tissue paper using decoupage technique.
  • Place LED lights inside painted bottles or jars to create decorative lamps or lanterns.

Tin Cans

  • Clean and remove labels from tin cans.
  • Use a hammer and nails to create patterns or designs on the surface.
  • Paint the cans in vibrant colors and use them as pencil holders, planters, or candle holders.

Old Fabric or Clothing

  • Cut old fabric or clothing into strips or squares.
  • Braid or weave the fabric strips to create rugs, placemats, or coasters.
  • Sew fabric squares together to make patchwork quilts or pillow covers.

Wood Scraps

  • Sand and polish small wood scraps to create decorative coasters or trivets.
  • Paint or carve designs on wooden scraps and hang them as wall art.
  • Glue together wooden sticks or blocks to make rustic photo frames or jewelry organizers.

Broken Ceramics or Tiles

  • Use broken ceramics or tiles to mosaic surfaces like tabletops, planters, or stepping stones.
  • Arrange the broken pieces into patterns or pictures and secure them with tile adhesive or grout.
  • Seal the finished mosaic with a waterproof sealant to protect it from moisture.
